Timpana

Timpana is a traditional Maltese dish made by baking pasta in a rich meat sauce inside a shortcrust pastry.

Ingredients

Filling

  • 3 large, ripe tomatoes
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 400 gm pork mince
  • 100 gm pancetta
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 500 ml chicken stock
  • 1/2 tsp ground allspice
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 180 gm of chicken livers
  • 1 1/2 unsalted butter, diced
  • 300 gm straight macaroni pasta
  • 2 hard boiled eggs, peeled
  • 3 eggs
  • 80 gm parmesan cheese grated

Shortcrust Pastry

  • 450 gm plain flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 210gm unsalted butter, chilled and diced, plus some for greasing
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp milk

Steps

  1. Score the bottom of the tomatoes and grate them on a box grater.
  2. Sauté the onion and garlic in the oil in a saucepan over a medium heat for 5 minutes. Add the minced meat and pancetta and cook until the meat has browned and any moisture has evaporated. Add the tomatoes, tomato paste, stock, spices and some salt and pepper and bring to the boil. Cover with a lid and simmer the ragu for 45 minutes. Scrape into a bowl to cool.
  3. In a frying pan, sauté the liver in the butter over a medium heat until the pieces are seared all over but still pink in the middle. Season with salt and pepper.
  4. Boil the pasta in well-salted water for a few minutes less than the packet recommends. Drain and rinse with cold water to cool the pasta down.
  5. Whisk 3 eggs in a large mixing bowl, then stir in the parmesan. Mix in the pasta, followed by the ragu and chicken livers. The pasta should not be as wet as a normal pasta with sauce, but should not be too dry either. Refrigerate until completely chilled
  6. While the pasta mixture is chilling, make the pastry. Combine the flour, butter and salt in a bowl and rub together with your fingertips until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs or rough sand.
  7. Add the water to create a firm dough. Shape into a ball, then flatten into a disc. Cover in plastic wrap and chill in the refrigerator for 45 minutes.
  8. Preheat the oven to 180°C and butter a large, tall non-stick springform cake tin (about 10 cm high and 24 cm wide).
  9. Roll out the pastry on a floured surface to 5 mm (1/4 in) thick. Line the tin with the pastry, then trim off the excess and form the scraps into a ball to use for the lid.
  10. Fill the pastry shell with half the pasta mixture. Scatter with the wedges of boiled egg, and top with the remaining pasta.
  11. Roll out the remaining pastry and cover the pie. Trim off the excess pastry leaving a small border. Fold in and use a thumb to make decorative indentations around the edge. Whisk the egg with the milk and brush over the top of the timpana.
  12. Bake in the oven for 45 minutes, then run a knife around the edge of the pie, remove the springform sides and cook for another 15 minutes.
